Global COVID-19 Analysis

Introduction

The Covid-19 pandemic began around 2020 and is still currently ongoing till this very day. It is safe to say that covid has impacted the lives of almost every single person on the planet, in more than one way. The aim of this visualization is to anaylze and identify trends and patterns of Covid-19 up to the present day.

Total Covid-19 Cases In Every country

The total number of cases in each country is represented using this choropleth map. The darker coloured areas on the map represents countries in the world where there high numbers of cases of COVID-19. On the other hand, lighter coloured areas on the map indicate lower number of cases in that particular country. A filter to obtain a specific range of population was also included at the bottom left of the graph.

Number of Confirmed Cases vs Number of Deaths

From the bubbleplot, we can observe that there is a positive correlation between the number of confirmed COVID-19 cases and the total number of deaths. Countries like the United States, India, and Brazil all have a very high number of deaths and number of confirmed cases. This might also be due to the vast population that each of these countries have.

Total Number of Vaccinations In Each Country

As indicated by the map figure, the total number of vaccinations in every country is represented by a circular mark, and varies in size according to the number of vaccinations in that particular country. We can observe that India has the highest number of vaccinations, and this might be because the fact that they have a large population of citizens.

Negative Impacts of COVID-19

As shown in the pie-chart, these are 4 examples of how COVID-19 has negatively impacted the people all around the world. It can be seen that COVID-19 has caused many people to be extreme poverty due to the loss of jobs. The pandemic has also caused people to have more health diseases such as cardiovascular diseases and diabetes. The last factor, smoking, might have been used as a coping mechanism for many stressed individuals, causing the number of smokers to rise all around the world.
